# Retail-Profitability-and-Performance-Analysis
This project analyzes the performance of a retail business operating across multiple regions, product categories, and customer segments.  Despite strong sales performance, the business faces inconsistent profitability, with limited visibility into what is driving profits and losses.

The goal of this analysis is to:

Identify key drivers of profitability
Detect inefficiencies across products and regions
Provide actionable recommendations to improve performance

## Business Problem

The company is experiencing:

Growing revenue but unstable profit
Lack of clarity on which products are profitable
High discounting without understanding its impact
Increasing operational costs (e.g., shipping)

## Key Question:
What is driving profitability, and where should the business focus to improve performance?

## Objectives
Analyze revenue, profit, and profit margin trends
Identify high-performing vs loss-making products
Evaluate the impact of discounts on profitability
Assess regional performance and cost distribution
Recommend data-driven actions

## Tools & Technologies
Power BI → Data visualization & dashboarding
DAX → Calculated measures (Profit Margin, Loss-making products, etc.)
Excel / SQL (background) → Data preparation & querying

 ## Key Insights
* 1. Revenue Growth Does Not Translate to Profit Growth
Sales show a consistent upward trend
Profit grows at a slower rate
Profit margins fluctuate over time

 Indicates margin pressure and hidden inefficiencies

* 2. Discounting Negatively Impacts Profitability
Higher discounts correlate with lower profit
Some products are heavily discounted but remain unprofitable

 Suggests ineffective discount strategy

* 3. Shipping Costs Reduce Overall Profit
Certain regions incur significantly higher shipping costs
These costs directly impact net profitability

 Logistics is a major cost driver

* 4. Product Performance is Highly Uneven
A small number of products generate most of the profit
Many products contribute little or negative profit
300+ products are loss-making

 Indicates portfolio inefficiency

* 5. Regional Performance Varies Significantly
Some regions have strong sales but weak profit
Profit margins differ widely across regions

 Points to operational and pricing inconsistencies

* 6. Category-Level Imbalance
Technology category is the most profitable
Furniture underperforms in both profit and margin

 Not all categories contribute equally to business success

## Recommendations
* ✅ 1. Optimize Discount Strategy
 Reduce excessive discounting on low-margin products
 Apply targeted discounts only where profitable
* ✅ 2. Improve Cost Efficiency (Shipping)
Reassess logistics strategy in high-cost regions
Negotiate better shipping rates or adjust pricing
* ✅ 3. Eliminate or Fix Loss-Making Products.
 Discontinue consistently unprofitable products.
 Re-evaluate pricing or sourcing strategies.
* ✅ 4. Focus on High-Performing Categories.
 Invest more in Technology products.
 Expand high-margin product lines.
* ✅ 5. Regional Strategy Optimization.
  Investigate low-profit regions with high sales.
  Adjust pricing, logistics, or product mix.
* ✅ 6. Continuous Performance Monitoring
Track profit margin alongside revenue
Regularly review product-level profitability

 ## Key Takeaway

Revenue alone is not a reliable indicator of business success.
True performance lies in understanding and optimizing profitability drivers.
